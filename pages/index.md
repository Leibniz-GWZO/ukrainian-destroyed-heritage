<p style="color: black;">Since the beginning of the full-scale Russian invasion of Ukraine, many cities have suffered from drones, missiles, and artillery strikes. It is difficult to determine the total number of ruined buildings, but the estimated count is already more than 200,000. This number includes unique and valuable architectural landmarks, but very little number of them are officially recognized and protected by law. A major part of ruined heritage was not protected by law and is less likely to be restored in the future. Numerous buildings were destroyed before being even researched, and their value was not known.</p>

<p style="color: black;">Despite the combat zone is mostly in the eastern part of the state, missiles and drones cause the damage across the territory of Ukraine. Strikes happen even in the western part of the state, near the border of Poland. The geography of the losses covers mostly the big cities, but also includes small ones and villages, some of which had lost their only remarkable buildings.</p>

<p style="color: black;">The typology of damage is wide. The ruined buildings are residential houses, administrative offices, factories and warehouses, schools and hospitals, stores and markets, railway stations and airports. Some of them originate from the 19th century, while others can be recently finished.</p>

<p style="color: black;">The fate of buildings is also different. Some of the damaged structures have already been restored, whereas others were deemed unrepairable and have already been demolished. Many are left in ruins and their fate is unclear. As many historical buildings are not enlisted as cultural heritage, they don’t have much chance to be restored. The ones located in central parts of the cities are more likely to be demolished because the construction business is interested in new construction on the spot of historic buildings.</p>

<p style="color: black;">This list cannot be considered comprehensive, as numerous losses remain unreported, and any new construction is susceptible to destruction at any moment.</p>
